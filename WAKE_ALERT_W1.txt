COMMUTE GUARDIAN, WAKE ALERT (W1 SPIKE)
Written 15 Jul 2026. Covers the code currently on the branch, uncommitted.

=====================================================================
1. WHAT THE FEATURE IS
=====================================================================

The product goal: if you fall asleep on the train, the app wakes you
before your stop. It never tries to detect sleep. Instead it asks you
to prove you are awake, and silence is what triggers the escalation.

The full flow (when finished, slices W2 and W3):
  - One station before your destination (or a required interchange),
    the app speaks a check-in: "your stop is next, tap your earphones
    to show you're awake."
  - If you tap any earphone / headset button, or press the on-screen
    button, nothing else happens. Awake riders never hear the alarm.
  - If you do NOT respond, an alarm ladder climbs: a rising two-tone
    alarm starts quiet, gets louder every 15 seconds, adds vibration
    (Android only), and keeps repeating at full volume.
  - It stops on its own only one station PAST your stop, because at
    that point the normal overshoot announcement takes over.
  - Phone calls pause the ladder (on a call means awake). After the
    call, the app re-orients you: "the train passed X and Y, your
    stop is next."

What exists TODAY is the W1 platform spike: the audio and the
earphone-tap plumbing, driven by a simplified 3-rung test ladder you
trigger by hand with a button. The station-aware logic (when to fire,
the ceiling, the call handling) is the next slice.

Why the alarm is special audio, not a notification sound:
  - On Android it plays on the ALARM volume stream. Your media volume
    can be at zero and it still sounds, like a morning alarm clock.
  - On iOS it plays as playback audio, so the silent switch and Focus
    modes cannot mute it, same trick navigation apps use.
  - It deliberately does not duck or pause your music. It plays OVER
    it. An alarm that politely lowers your podcast is an alarm you
    sleep through.

The pieces in the code:
  - assets/audio/wake_alarm.wav: the tone. Generated by
    tool/build_alarm_tone.py, never hand-edit it, rerun the script.
  - lib/services/wake_alert_spike.dart: the throwaway test ladder.
  - MainActivity.kt: the Android media session that catches your
    earphone tap while the ladder is live.
  - ios/Runner/AppDelegate.swift: the iOS equivalent. NOT yet
    compiled or tested (needs the next IPA cycle).

=====================================================================
2. HOW THE TEST LADDER RUNS (TIMINGS)
=====================================================================

t = 0:00   Check-in speech through the earphones: "Wake alert test.
           Your stop is next. Tap your earphones, or press the I am
           awake button."
t = 0:25   Rung 1: alarm tone starts at 30 percent volume, plus a
           firm spoken "Wake up. Your stop is next."
t = 0:40   Rung 2: tone at 60 percent, plus vibration (Android).
t = 0:55   Rung 3: tone at FULL volume, plus vibration.
t = 1:10+  Repeats at full volume with vibration every 15 seconds.
t = 2:00   Safety timeout: the spike stands down by itself so a
           forgotten bench test does not blast forever. The real
           feature will instead stop one station past your stop.

Acknowledging at ANY point stands the whole ladder down immediately
and the app says "Good, you are awake."

Ways to acknowledge:
  1. Tap your earphones (any media button: play, pause, next, a TWS
     tap, an inline wire clicker). This is the one only you can test,
     it needs a real human finger on real hardware.
  2. Press the cream "I'm awake" button that appears on screen while
     the ladder is live. This is the guaranteed fallback.

=====================================================================
3. HOW TO TEST IT ON THE 3T
=====================================================================

Note: the 3T currently has the RELEASE build installed. This work is
newer than that build, so first install a fresh debug build:

  flutter install
      (phone plugged in, or: flutter build apk --debug, then
       adb install -r build/app/outputs/flutter-apk/app-debug.apk)

Then:

  1. Connect earphones (the TWS you ride with is the honest test).
  2. Open the app and start any journey, for example Shahad to
     Kalyan. The wake test needs a running ride because the ladder
     lives inside the Travel Mode service, exactly where the real
     feature will run.
  3. Tap "Test wake alert" (next to "Test announcement").
  4. Lock the screen and pocket the phone. This is the point of the
     test: the ladder must climb with the screen off.
  5. Let it climb at least two rungs, then tap your earphones.
     Expected: tone stops instantly, "Good, you are awake."
  6. Run it again and let it hit the 2 minute timeout to hear the
     full ramp once.

What to check while it runs, in rough order of importance:
  - Earphone tap stands it down, from every rung, screen locked.
  - Media volume at ZERO, alarm still audible (that proves the
    ALARM stream routing, the whole point of the spike).
    Alarm volume slider is the one that changes its loudness.
  - Music playing (Spotify or similar) when the ladder fires: the
    tone should play OVER the music, and your earphone tap should
    reach US (stand down the alert), not pause the music. If the
    tap pauses the music instead, that is a finding, log it.
  - Vibration arrives on rungs 2 and up.
  - After stand-down, your earphone tap controls your music again
    (the app releases the media buttons when no ladder is live).
  - Everything appears in the debug log as WAKE lines, and in the
    pulled log file for the session.

iPhone: do NOT expect this to work yet. The Swift side has never
been compiled (no Mac in the loop). It rides the next IPA per the
rationing policy; test the silent switch and AirPods tap then.

=====================================================================
4. ENABLE / DISABLE
=====================================================================

There is no setting yet, and nothing fires on its own. In W1 the
ladder ONLY starts when you press "Test wake alert" during a running
ride. Ways it stops:

  - Tap your earphones (any media button), or
  - Press the on-screen "I'm awake" button, or
  - Wait out the 2 minute safety timeout, or
  - End the journey (hold to confirm), which tears the ladder down
    with the rest of Travel Mode.

So "disable" today = simply do not press the test button. Once the
real engine lands (W2/W3) the trigger becomes automatic near your
destination, and a proper user-facing toggle belongs to the Phase 2
product UI.

If the tone itself is ever wrong (pitch, speed, length), edit the
constants in tool/build_alarm_tone.py and rerun:

  python tool/build_alarm_tone.py

=====================================================================
5. KNOWN LIMITS OF THE SPIKE (ACCEPTED, NOT BUGS)
=====================================================================

  - The earphone tap is best effort. The media session lives in the
    app's activity; if Android has killed the backgrounded app UI,
    the tap is lost and the on-screen button is the fallback. The
    escalation itself is the safety net either way.
  - The 3-rung ladder is hardcoded test logic. Real timings and the
    station-aware trigger arrive with the W2 engine.
  - iOS is written but unverified until the next IPA.
